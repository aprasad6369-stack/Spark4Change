About the Project

Local businesses are the backbone of San Diego's economy. When businesses close, it doesn't just affect owners‚Äîit leads to unemployment, weakens community stability, and disrupts the fabric of our neighborhoods. Our project explores the vital connection between business vitality and unemployment, providing data-driven insights to strengthen our local economy.

Key Focus: Understanding how business closures impact unemployment and community wellbeing in San Diego through comprehensive data analysis and machine learning.

Our Methodology

1. Data Collection

Gather comprehensive business and unemployment data from multiple verified sources

2. Data Preprocessing

Handle missing values, perform encoding, and conduct feature selection for optimal model performance

3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Spatial Mapping: Identify business closure hotspots across neighborhoods
Time Series Analysis: Analyze trends in business creation and closure, forecast future patterns

4. Machine Learning Models

Logistic Regression: Predict business closure probability
Random Forest: Enhanced predictions with deeper insights and higher accuracy
Clustering: Identify main business ecosystems across San Diego
