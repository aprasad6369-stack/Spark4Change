When Businesses Thrive, Communities Survive

The Dynamics of Local Business Survival and Social Impact in San Diego

Prepared by Spark 4Change · San Diego · 30 Sep 2025

Local businesses are the backbone of San Diego’s economy. When they close, unemployment rises and community stability is threatened. Our project explores how business vitality connects to unemployment and what we can do about it.

Project Snapshot

  • Region: San Diego
  • Focus: Local business survival & unemployment
  • Approach: Spatial analysis, time-series trends, ML models

We combine open data, exploratory analysis, and predictive modeling to inform policies and tools that help businesses stay open and people stay employed.

Project Overview

Why it matters

Local businesses generate jobs, services, and neighborhood identity for San Diego. When they shut down, the effects ripple outward: higher unemployment, reduced access to services, and increased economic stress for already vulnerable communities.

This project connects business vitality to unemployment, highlighting where communities are most at risk and how targeted support can make a difference.

Goals

  • Analyze business survival trends in San Diego, including openings, closures, and patterns over time.
  • Study how business closures impact unemployment, especially in vulnerable neighborhoods.
  • Provide insights for policies and tools that strengthen the local economy and support communities.

Expected Outcomes

Outcome A

Training & Knowledge Access

  • Launch training programs on business policies, financial literacy, digital marketing, and crisis management.
  • Provide clear guidance for navigating permits, taxes, and government support programs.
  • Translate complex regulations into plain-language guides that busy owners can use.
Outcome B

A User-Friendly Online Platform

  • Build a central website hub where entrepreneurs and workers can:
    • Learn about business policies in accessible language.
    • Access step-by-step training modules (videos, toolkits, and checklists).
    • Connect with grants, loans, and workforce programs.
    • Track business survival tips, legal updates, and local resources.
Outcome C

Workforce Training & Employment Links

  • Offer job-matching tools to connect unemployed residents with local businesses that are hiring.
  • Create apprenticeships and upskilling courses to prepare workers for shifts in local industries.
  • Align training content with the sectors most at risk of closures to reduce unemployment before it spikes.

Data & Methodology

Data Collection & Preparation
  • Gather open data on population, unemployment, and businesses.
  • Handle missing values, encode categories, and select features.
Exploratory Analysis

Spatial Mapping

Map the location of businesses and closures to identify geographic hotspots—areas where communities may be losing access to jobs and essential services.

Time-Series Analysis

Analyze trends in business creation and closure over time, and forecast future changes to anticipate risk.

Machine Learning
  • Logistic Regression – Predict whether a particular business is likely to close.
  • Random Forest – Improve prediction accuracy and uncover deeper insights into risk factors.
  • Clustering – Discover main business ecosystems in San Diego (e.g., neighborhood-level or sector-level patterns).
